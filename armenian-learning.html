4 New Letters — The Vowels of Armenian
✓ Done
▼

Today's 4 letters are all vowels — the backbone of Armenian pronunciation. They cover the most common vowel sounds you'll encounter. Master these and reading becomes dramatically easier.

Ե
ե
Yech
"ye" at start · "e" in middle
At the start of a word: "ye" like yes. Mid-word: "e" like bed. This dual behavior is key — context tells you which.
Զ
զ
Za
"z" — like zoo
A clean "z" sound. The uppercase Զ looks like a stylized number 2. Lowercase զ is distinctive — a looping descender. Think: Za → zoo.
Է
է
Eh
"e" — like bed
Always a pure "e" — unlike Ե. Crucially: Է is also the word for "is" in Eastern Armenian. "Ան Է" = "He/she is." You already know a verb!
Ը
ը
Et
"uh" — the schwa /ə/
The neutral "uh" sound — like the "a" in about or the "e" in the. Extremely common in Armenian. Think of it as the "lazy" vowel — mouth relaxed, neutral position.
⚡ Key Contrast: Ե vs Է vs Ը

These three look similar but sound different. Say them one after another:

Ե
ye / e
≠
Է
e (always)
≠
Ը
uh (schwa)

Say all three aloud 3 times in a row. The mouth position shifts slightly for each — feel the difference.

Weekly Roadmap — Alphabet in 10 Days
Days 1–2
Ա Բ Գ Դ Ե Զ Է Ը
First 8 letters. Focus on recognizing vowels: Ա (a), Ե (ye), Է (e), Ը (uh).
Days 3–4
Թ Ժ Ի Լ Խ Ծ Կ
Consonants including the uniquely Armenian sounds Խ (kh) and Ծ (ts).
Days 5–6
Հ Ձ Ղ Ճ Մ Յ Ն
Mid-alphabet. Learn Ձ (dz), Ղ (gh) — sounds unique to Armenian.
Days 7–8
Շ Ո Չ Պ Ջ Ռ Ս
Includes Շ (sh), Չ (ch), Ջ (j) — familiar sounds in new shapes.
Days 9–10
Վ Տ Ր Ց Ու Փ Ք Եվ Օ Ֆ
Final letters + ligatures. Review the full alphabet, write simple words.
Week 2+
Reading & Words
